# Investment-Grade Executive Summary - AI Prompt Upgrade

## Problem Statement

User requested: "Generate a high-quality Executive Summary for a real estate development decision that meets institutional investment standards."

Previous output was too generic with phrases like "favorable potential" and "further analysis recommended".

## Solution: Professional Investment Analyst Prompt

### New AI System Prompt

The AI now acts as a **Senior Investment Analyst at a top-tier real estate development firm** preparing summaries for site acquisition committees.

### Key Improvements

#### 1. Quality Standards Enforced

DECISION Section must:
- State verdict clearly (GO/NO-GO/CONDITIONAL)
- Justify using SPECIFIC metrics (ROI %, soil score, FAR, etc.)
- Reference concrete data from analysis
- Cite at least 2 specific metrics or data points
- FORBIDDEN: "favorable potential", "various factors", "generally positive"

STRENGTH Section must:
- Identify ONE most compelling advantage
- Include quantifiable evidence (e.g., "FAR of 2.5 enables 15,000 sqm buildable area")
- Use numbers, scores, or concrete factors
- FORBIDDEN: "good location", "strong potential", "favorable conditions"

RISK Section must:
- Identify SINGLE most critical threat
- Explain actual impact (cost, timeline, feasibility)
- Be realistic and sharp
- FORBIDDEN: "potential concerns", "further investigation needed"

ACTION Section must:
- Give ONE clear, strategic next step
- Make it actionable and time-bound when possible
- FORBIDDEN: "conduct further research", "consider options", "evaluate factors"
- REQUIRED: Specific task (e.g., "Commission geotechnical boring at 15m depth intervals")

#### 2. Professional Tone Requirements

- Write like presenting to a CFO or investment committee
- Crisp, confident, evidence-based
- No hedging or vague qualifiers
- Each sentence must add value
- 100-120 words total

#### 3. Example Excellence Standard

The prompt includes a detailed example showing the expected quality:

"DECISION: GO - Risk-adjusted ROI of 18.2% significantly exceeds our 12% hurdle rate, driven by zoning code R-4 permitting 3-story residential with FAR 0.85, translating to 4,200 sqm buildable area on this 4,950 sqm site.

STRENGTH: Soil stability score of 89/100 with groundwater depth at 12m enables shallow-spread foundation, reducing structural costs by $285K compared to pile foundation and compressing construction timeline to 8 months.

RISK: Market absorption risk in Princeton's luxury residential segment, where 14-month average days-on-market and 8% vacancy rate could extend capital recovery by 6-9 months beyond pro forma.

ACTION: Execute Letter of Intent at $32/sqft land basis (8% below comparable $35/sqft), contingent on Phase I environmental clearance and title verification within 45 days."

### Comparison: Before vs After

BEFORE (Generic):
"DECISION: GO - This site shows favorable development potential.
STRENGTH: Good zoning parameters and location.
RISK: Some site-specific concerns require verification.
ACTION: Conduct further analysis before proceeding."

AFTER (Investment-Grade):
"DECISION: GO - Risk-adjusted ROI of 16.3% exceeds 12% hurdle rate, with R-5 zoning enabling 3,850 sqm buildable area via FAR 0.75 on 5,130 sqm parcel.
STRENGTH: Soil stability score of 91/100 with 14m groundwater depth permits shallow-spread foundation, reducing structural costs by $320K and enabling 35-day foundation phase.
RISK: High groundwater seasonality requires dewatering system at $85K capital cost plus $12K monthly operating expense during 8-month construction window.
ACTION: Secure geotechnical boring report with 15m depth sampling and negotiate land acquisition below $30/sqft to maintain 16%+ ROI threshold."

### Data-Driven Analysis

The AI now has access to specific context:
- Site area in square meters
- Geological risk level and soil stability score
- Soil type and groundwater depth
- Zoning code, district, max height, max floors, FAR
- Foundation type, cost, and feasibility score
- Gross ROI, risk-adjusted ROI, total cost, revenue
- Investment grade rating

Every recommendation must reference these concrete metrics.
